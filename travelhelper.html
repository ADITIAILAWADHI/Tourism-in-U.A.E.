WHEN TO VISIT UAE?

The months from mid-September to December comes under the Autumn season in the UAE. Temperature varying from approximately 35 degrees (humid and hot) during days and a bit colder during nights. Heat is controlled in this season due to the mild rain. ...This season is considered suitable for visitors and residents to participate in outdoor activities. UAE starts welcoming tourists in this season as it celebrates UAE National Day in December with variety of hotel and restaurant offers and long weekend, which attracts the tourists.



Springs are from December to May in the UAE. Temperature varies from 27 degrees in December during the day, drops to 15 degrees during the night (the coldest weather in the UAE with high speed breeze), typical rainfall and 40 degrees in the May. Heat is no more an issue in this season. ...UAE gets most of its tourists in this season as this is consisdered the most relaxing season of the UAE with variations of temperrature. UAE becomes busiest during this season due to many festivals. It is highly recommended to visit UAE in this season for both outdoor and indoor activities.



May to mid- September are considered the most hottest months of the UAE. It is not recommended to visit UAE in this season for outdoor activities, only recommended for indoor activities. The temperature even reaches over 50 degrees in this season due to hot and dry weather, global warming, high levels of humidity and dusty days. Due to rare rain, it becomes unmanageable to spend summers outdoor. ... Indoor activities like Malls, Indoor theme parks and ice parks are popular in this season but make sure to carry sunscreens, umbrellas, hats and sunglasses with you for day out.

CULTURE AND LIFESTYLE

UAE's culture is firmly rooted in Arabia's Islamic traditions. Islam is more than a religion; it is a way of life that governs everyday events from what to wear to what to eat and drink. The UAE's culture and heritage is inextricably linked to its religion, and it is a shining example of Islam's true commitment to tolerance and hospitality. ... Foreigners are free to practise their own religion and the dress code is liberal. Women are able to drive and walk around unescorted. Among the most highly prized virtues are courtesy and hospitality, and visitors are sure to be charmed by the genuine friendliness of the people.

Lightweight summer clothing is suitable for most of the year, but something slightly warmer may be needed for the winter months. Be sure to take some sort of jacket or sweater when visiting hotels or the cinema, as the air conditioning can be fierce. Malls, health clubs and resort facilities are generally more accepting of what's fashionable, but when visiting government offices it is best to cover your shoulders and legs. ... It is especially recommended that you dress more conservatively during Ramadan. In the evenings, restaurants and clubs usually have a mixt of western, Arabic and Asian styles. Again, ladies are advised to take a pashmina or jacket because of cold air conditioning.
